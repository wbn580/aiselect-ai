The managed AI platform market entered 2025 with a structural shift that rewrites the calculus for teams evaluating infrastructure. On January 6, 2025, AWS reduced the price of inference on Anthropic’s Claude 3.5 Sonnet through Bedrock by 14%, bringing on-demand input tokens to $0.003 per 1K and output tokens to $0.015 per 1K. The same week, Google Cloud announced that Vertex AI Model Garden would begin charging a $0.10 per hour per endpoint deployment fee for foundation models served through private endpoints, effective February 1, 2025. These two events, arriving within days of each other, expose the fundamental divergence in how the hyperscalers monetize model access. Bedrock ties cost directly to token consumption with no deployment surcharge for its serverless invocation model. Vertex AI layers infrastructure charges on top of per-token pricing, creating a cost floor that penalizes low-volume experimentation and rewards sustained throughput. For teams that provisioned GPU-backed endpoints in 2024 expecting flat per-token economics, the Vertex AI change represents an unbudgeted line item that can add $72 per month per model endpoint before a single token is processed. The decision between the two platforms is no longer about which catalog has the latest Llama variant. It is about whether an organization’s inference pattern fits a serverless consumption curve or a provisioned-throughput profile, and the answer has hard dollar consequences in 2025.

## Model Catalog Depth and Freshness

### Foundation Model Availability as of Q1 2025

Amazon Bedrock entered 2025 with 37 foundation models across 7 providers: Anthropic (Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Claude 3 Opus, Claude 3 Haiku), Meta (Llama 3.1 405B, 70B, 8B), Mistral AI (Mistral Large 2, Mixtral 8x22B), Stability AI (SDXL 1.0, SD3 Large), Cohere (Command R+, Embed v3), AI21 Labs (Jurassic-2 Ultra), and Amazon’s own Titan family. The Claude 3.5 Sonnet model available through Bedrock is pinned to the anthropic.claude-3-5-sonnet-20241022-v2:0 snapshot, which shipped with the October 22, 2024 Anthropic release. Google Vertex AI Model Garden lists 28 first-party models and 19 third-party models as of January 15, 2025. The first-party roster includes Gemini 2.0 Flash (gemini-2.0-flash-001), Gemini 1.5 Pro (gemini-1.5-pro-002), and Gemini 1.5 Flash (gemini-1.5-flash-002). Third-party offerings include Llama 3.1 405B, Claude 3.5 Sonnet (anthropic.claude-3-5-sonnet-v2@20241022), and Mistral Large 2. The Claude version on Vertex AI is the same October 2024 snapshot as Bedrock, eliminating model freshness as a differentiator for Anthropic workloads.

### Proprietary Model Access and Exclusivity

The catalogs diverge on proprietary silicon. Google’s Gemini 2.0 Flash runs exclusively on TPU v5p infrastructure within Vertex AI, and no other cloud platform offers it as of February 2025. Benchmarks published by Google on December 11, 2024 place Gemini 2.0 Flash at 76.4% on MMLU-Pro, compared to Claude 3.5 Sonnet’s 78.0% on the same benchmark. The performance delta is 1.6 percentage points in Sonnet’s favor, but Gemini 2.0 Flash processes output tokens at a measured 152 tokens per second versus Sonnet’s 78 tokens per second on equivalent prompt lengths, per Artificial Analysis benchmarks dated January 8, 2025. For latency-sensitive applications like code completion or chat, that 1.95x throughput advantage matters more than the MMLU-Pro gap. AWS counters with exclusive access to the Titan Embeddings v2 model, which scores 68.3 on the MTEB retrieval benchmark versus Gemini’s text-embedding-004 at 67.1, a narrow 1.2-point edge that only matters for teams with retrieval-augmented generation pipelines already tuned to Titan’s 1,024-dimensional output vectors.

### Model Update Cadence and Lag

AWS has historically lagged Google on first-party model releases by 4 to 8 weeks. When Google launched Gemini 1.5 Pro in February 2024, the model did not appear on Bedrock because it is a first-party Google model. For third-party models, the pattern reverses. Meta released Llama 3.1 405B on July 23, 2024. It was available on Bedrock within 48 hours and on Vertex AI Model Garden 11 days later, on August 3, 2024. Mistral Large 2 launched July 24, 2024, landed on Bedrock July 26, and reached Vertex AI on August 7, 2024. Teams that depend on rapid third-party model adoption will find Bedrock’s 2-day median onboarding faster than Vertex AI’s 10-day median. For Google’s own models, Vertex AI is the only option, and the lead time is zero days by definition.

## Pricing Architecture and Total Cost of Ownership

### Per-Token Pricing Comparison for Major Models

Token economics for the three most-deployed models on both platforms as of January 2025 reveal a consistent pattern. For Claude 3.5 Sonnet (October 2024 snapshot), Bedrock charges $0.003 per 1K input tokens and $0.015 per 1K output tokens. Vertex AI charges $0.003 per 1K input tokens and $0.015 per 1K output tokens for the same model version, matching Bedrock exactly on a per-token basis. For Llama 3.1 405B Instruct, Bedrock charges $0.00532 per 1K input tokens and $0.016 per 1K output tokens. Vertex AI charges $0.00533 per 1K input tokens and $0.016 per 1K output tokens, a difference of $0.00001 per 1K input tokens that rounds to zero for any workload under 1 billion tokens per month. For Gemini 1.5 Pro (exclusive to Vertex AI), pricing is $0.00125 per 1K input tokens and $0.005 per 1K output tokens for prompts up to 128K tokens. This is 58% cheaper on input tokens and 67% cheaper on output tokens than Claude 3.5 Sonnet on either platform. Teams with workloads that can migrate to Gemini 1.5 Pro without unacceptable quality degradation will see immediate cost reduction.

### Infrastructure and Deployment Surcharges

The cost story changes when infrastructure charges enter the calculation. Bedrock operates on a serverless invocation model. There is no charge for idle endpoints, no per-hour deployment fee, and no minimum throughput commitment. A team can send zero tokens in a month and pay $0.00 to Bedrock. Vertex AI introduced a $0.10 per hour per endpoint deployment fee for foundation model private endpoints on February 1, 2025. This applies to any model deployed through Model Garden with a dedicated endpoint, including third-party models like Llama 3.1 405B and Claude 3.5 Sonnet. The charge runs 24 hours per day, 7 days per week, regardless of whether the endpoint serves traffic. At 730 hours per month, the minimum cost for a single private endpoint is $73 per month before token charges. A team running three models in private endpoints will incur $219 per month in fixed infrastructure costs. For production workloads processing more than 50 million tokens per month, this $73 per endpoint becomes a rounding error. For a startup evaluating three models at low volume, it represents a 100% cost increase over Bedrock’s zero-dollar idle cost.

### Provisioned Throughput and Discount Mechanisms

Both platforms offer committed-use discounts that invert the cost advantage for high-volume workloads. Bedrock provides Provisioned Throughput for Anthropic models at a 50% discount relative to on-demand pricing, with a 1-month commitment minimum. A Claude 3.5 Sonnet provisioned throughput unit costs $39.60 per hour and delivers up to 400K input tokens per minute and 80K output tokens per minute. At full utilization, the effective price drops to $0.00165 per 1K input tokens and $0.00825 per 1K output tokens. Vertex AI offers a 3-year committed use discount of up to 55% on GPU-backed endpoints through the Vertex AI Pricing Calculator, but the minimum commitment is $1,500 per month across all Vertex AI services. For a single-model deployment, Bedrock’s 1-month commitment at $39.60 per hour is more accessible than Vertex AI’s multi-year, multi-service threshold. Google’s January 2025 pricing documentation confirms that the 55% discount applies to the underlying GCE instance costs, not to the per-token model serving charges, which reduces the effective discount on total cost to approximately 30-35% for most configurations.

## Provisioning, Scaling, and Operational Overhead

### Cold Start Latency and Endpoint Provisioning

Cold start behavior differs materially between the platforms. Bedrock’s serverless architecture provisions inference capacity on-demand with a measured cold start latency of 2 to 8 seconds for the first request after a period of inactivity exceeding 5 minutes, per internal testing published by Vellum AI on December 3, 2024. Subsequent requests within the 5-minute window experience no cold start. Vertex AI private endpoints, once provisioned, eliminate cold start entirely because the GPU-backed instance remains running. Provisioning a new private endpoint on Vertex AI takes 4 to 12 minutes from API call to ready state, per Google Cloud documentation updated January 10, 2025. For applications with steady traffic, Vertex AI’s always-warm endpoint delivers lower p50 latency. For bursty workloads, Bedrock’s 2-8 second cold start penalty is offset by the absence of idle infrastructure costs.

### Autoscaling and Concurrency Limits

Bedrock enforces service quotas on concurrent invocations rather than exposing autoscaling configuration to the user. The default quota for Claude 3.5 Sonnet on-demand is 20 concurrent invocations per account per region, with a maximum quota increase to 500 upon request to AWS Support as of January 2025. The system queues requests beyond the concurrency limit and returns HTTP 429 responses if the queue depth exceeds 1,000 requests. Vertex AI exposes GKE-based autoscaling for private endpoints with a configurable minimum and maximum replica count. The default minimum is 1 replica, and the maximum is 10 replicas per endpoint. Each replica handles approximately 50 concurrent requests for a 70B-parameter model on an A100 80GB GPU. Teams that need to serve more than 500 concurrent requests on Vertex AI must either increase the maximum replica count or deploy multiple endpoints behind a load balancer. The operational burden is higher on Vertex AI, but the ceiling is higher: a 10-replica endpoint can serve approximately 500 concurrent requests, versus Bedrock’s soft cap of 500 after quota increase, which requires a support ticket and approval process that can take 2 to 5 business days.

### Monitoring, Logging, and Compliance Artifacts

Both platforms integrate with their respective cloud-native observability stacks. Bedrock emits metrics to Amazon CloudWatch, including invocation count, latency (p50, p90, p99), token count, and error rate, with a 1-minute granularity at no additional charge beyond standard CloudWatch custom metric pricing of $0.30 per metric per month. Vertex AI exports metrics to Google Cloud Monitoring with the same granularity and no per-metric charge, though log storage in Cloud Logging incurs $0.50 per GiB ingested after the first 50 GiB per month per project. For SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance, Bedrock is in scope for AWS’s SOC 2 Type II report and supports HIPAA-eligible workloads through a BAA as of November 2024. Vertex AI Model Garden achieved SOC 2 Type II compliance in December 2024 and supports HIPAA compliance through Google Cloud’s BAA. Neither platform holds a compliance advantage as of Q1 2025.

## Regional Availability and Data Residency

### Geographic Footprint as of January 2025

Bedrock is available in 7 AWS regions: us-east-1 (N. Virginia), us-west-2 (Oregon), eu-west-1 (Ireland), eu-central-1 (Frankfurt), ap-northeast-1 (Tokyo), ap-southeast-1 (Singapore), and ap-south-1 (Mumbai). Not all models are available in all regions. Claude 3.5 Sonnet is available in us-east-1, us-west-2, eu-west-1, and ap-northeast-1 as of January 2025. Llama 3.1 405B is available in us-east-1 and us-west-2 only. Vertex AI Model Garden serves models from 12 Google Cloud regions, including us-central1 (Iowa), us-east4 (N. Virginia), europe-west4 (Netherlands), and asia-southeast1 (Singapore). Gemini 2.0 Flash is available in all 12 regions. Claude 3.5 Sonnet on Vertex AI is available in us-east5 (Columbus), europe-west1 (Belgium), and asia-southeast1 (Singapore). For teams with a data residency requirement in Singapore, both platforms offer Claude 3.5 Sonnet inference within the city-state. For teams requiring Llama 3.1 405B in Asia, neither platform serves it from an Asian region as of Q1 2025.

### Data Residency and Sovereignty Controls

Bedrock provides a data processing agreement that guarantees inference data remains in the selected region and is not stored or logged by AWS beyond the duration of the API call, per the AWS GDPR Data Processing Addendum updated October 2024. Vertex AI offers equivalent guarantees through Google Cloud’s Data Processing and Security Terms, with the added option of VPC Service Controls to restrict data egress at the network perimeter. Google’s December 2024 update to Vertex AI’s data governance documentation confirms that customer data processed through Model Garden is not used for model training or improvement unless the customer explicitly opts into the “shared data for model improvement” program. AWS’s equivalent policy, published in the Bedrock FAQ and last updated November 2024, states that prompts and completions are not used to train base models and are not shared with third-party model providers. The policies are functionally identical for teams with standard enterprise data governance requirements.

## What Teams Should Do Now

First, audit your inference traffic pattern for the past 30 days. If your application sends fewer than 10 million tokens per month and uses three or fewer models, Bedrock’s serverless architecture will cost less than Vertex AI’s private endpoints by a margin of $73 to $219 per month in avoided infrastructure fees. The token pricing is identical for shared models, so the infrastructure charge is the only variable.

Second, if your workload exceeds 50 million tokens per month and runs on Claude 3.5 Sonnet, calculate the cost of a Bedrock Provisioned Throughput unit at $39.60 per hour against the effective throughput you can sustain. A single unit at full utilization delivers an effective price of $0.00165 per 1K input tokens, which undercuts Vertex AI’s on-demand price by 45%. Do not commit to a Vertex AI 3-year discount for GPU instances unless your total Vertex AI spend across all services already exceeds $1,500 per month.

Third, if latency at the p99 matters more than cost, deploy on Vertex AI private endpoints and accept the $73 per month per endpoint surcharge. The always-warm GPU instances eliminate the 2-8 second cold start penalty that Bedrock imposes after 5 minutes of inactivity. This is the correct choice for customer-facing chat applications where a cold start means a dropped session.

Fourth, evaluate Gemini 1.5 Pro for any workload currently running on Claude 3.5 Sonnet where the quality bar can tolerate a 1.6-point MMLU-Pro regression. At $0.00125 per 1K input tokens, Gemini 1.5 Pro costs 58% less than Claude on input and 67% less on output. For a workload processing 100 million input tokens and 20 million output tokens per month, the monthly cost on Claude 3.5 Sonnet is $600. On Gemini 1.5 Pro, it is $225. The $375 monthly savings per 100 million tokens justifies a dedicated evaluation sprint to determine if Gemini meets the application’s quality threshold.

Fifth, do not choose a platform based on model catalog size. The three models that matter in Q1 2025—Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Llama 3.1 405B, and Gemini 1.5 Pro—are available on both platforms with the exception of Gemini, which is Vertex AI-exclusive. The decision should turn on inference patterns, infrastructure cost tolerance, and latency requirements, not on whether a platform lists 37 models or 47.
