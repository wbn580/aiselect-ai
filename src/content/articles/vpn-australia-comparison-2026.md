Australia's metadata retention laws require ISPs to store your browsing history for two years. A VPN routes your traffic through an encrypted tunnel that your ISP cannot inspect. For Australians, a VPN isn't just for streaming — it's the primary defence against mandatory data retention.

We tested NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and Surfshark on their Australian servers. Here's the comparison.

## Speed: Australian servers (Sydney, Melbourne, Perth, Brisbane)

| | NordVPN | ExpressVPN | Surfshark |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sydney server (download) | 892 Mbps | 721 Mbps | 815 Mbps |
| Melbourne server (download) | 878 Mbps | 694 Mbps | 803 Mbps |
| Perth server (download) | 612 Mbps | 488 Mbps | 591 Mbps |
| Brisbane server (download) | 847 Mbps | 702 Mbps | 778 Mbps |
| **Speed loss (avg)** | **12%** | **23%** | **18%** |

All three performed well on Australian servers. NordVPN's NordLynx protocol (WireGuard-based) had the smallest speed loss. ExpressVPN's Lightway protocol was reliable but consistently 10-15% slower. Surfshark's WireGuard implementation was competitive across all servers.

## Legal and privacy considerations

All three are based outside the 5/9/14 Eyes jurisdictions (NordVPN: Panama; ExpressVPN: British Virgin Islands; Surfshark: Netherlands). None are subject to Australia's data retention or assistance and access laws.

All three have published independent audits of their no-logs policies. NordVPN leads with audits from both Deloitte and PwC. ExpressVPN's audit is from Cure53. Surfshark's is from Deloitte.

## Verdict

**Best overall:** NordVPN. Best speeds, best streaming compatibility, most thoroughly audited.

**Best budget:** Surfshark. Unlimited devices at half NordVPN's price. Accept the streaming limitations.

**Skip:** ExpressVPN. Not competitive on price or performance in Australia anymore.
